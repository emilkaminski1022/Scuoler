Practicing computer ethics
Searching the Internet is often the fastest and easiest way to gather information. For example, you can use the Internet to search for information for your school assignments or an office presentation. You can also download songs and movies from websites. You do not have to pay to download information from most of the websites, however, these downloads might not actually be free. The information in a website is legally owned by the author who created it or by the website that published it. Therefore, you might need permission from the author or the owner of the website to use the content. You need to be aware of the rights and required permissions before downloading any content from a website, including the following types of content:
  • Songs
  • Movies
  • Software
  • Pictures
This module explains the meaning of intellectual property in the field of computing, and how unauthorized use of intellectual property can lead to copyright violation. You will also learn about the various legal concerns associated with information exchange.
